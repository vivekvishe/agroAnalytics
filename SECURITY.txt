FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Q: Is the hash secure enough?
A: Yes! SHA-256 is cryptographically secure. It's used by:
   - Bitcoin and blockchain
   - SSL/TLS certificates
   - Password managers
   - Government systems

Q: Can someone crack the hash?
A: Theoretically yes, but practically no. It would take:
   - Billions of years with current computers
   - Trying trillions of password combinations
   - Not worth the effort for most attackers

Q: What if someone finds the hash on GitHub?
A: They still can't log in! They need the original password.
   The hash alone is useless for authentication.

Q: Should I change the password now?
A: Only if you suspect it's been compromised. Otherwise,
   the hash provides adequate security.

Q: Can I use special characters in the password?
A: Yes! Any Unicode character works. Examples:
   - MyP@ssw0rd!2024
   - Contraseña#Segura
   - パスワード123

Q: How often should I change the password?
A: Every 90 days is recommended for production systems.
   For personal use, change when you suspect compromise.
